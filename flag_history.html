History of the Irish Flag

The Irish flag, known as the **tricolour**, consists of three vertical stripes: **green, white, and orange**. It was first flown in **1848** by Thomas Francis Meagher as a symbol of unity between Irish Catholics and Protestants.

The **green** represents the Gaelic tradition of Ireland, **orange** symbolizes the Protestant supporters of William of Orange, and **white** stands for peace between both communities.

Officially adopted as the national flag in **1922**, it remains a symbol of Ireland’s history, identity, and hope for unity.
